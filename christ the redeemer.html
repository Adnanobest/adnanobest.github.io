Christ the Redeemer
Introduction Historical Significance Interesting Facts

Introduction

  • Location: At the summit of Mount Corcovado, overlooking Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
  • Architecture: Stands 98 feet (30 meters) tall, and it's the largest Art Deco-style sculpture globally. It was completed in 1931. It was Constructed from reinforced concrete and soapstone.
  • Symbolism: Represents Jesus Christ with outstretched arms, symbolizing peace and protection.
  • Views: Offers breathtaking panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro and Guanabara Bay.

Historical Significance

  • A symbol of faith, hope, and Brazilian culture.
  • Recognized as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
  • A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Interesting Facts

  • Located in the Tijuca Forest National Forest, atop the Corcovado Mountain.
  • Weather Hazard: Being perched on a mountain makes the statue susceptible to lightning strikes. It gets hit several times a year. In 2008, a thunderstorm damaged the head, eyebrows, and fingers. Later, in 2014, lightning struck and broke one of Christ's fingers.
  • Original Design: The statue we know today is different from its original plan. Initially, it was meant to depict a large Christ holding a globe and a cross. However, the final design features outstretched arms, symbolizing peace and protection.
  • Gradual Darkening: Over time, the statue will darken. The light-colored stone, sourced from a quarry near Ouro Preto in Minas Gerais, is challenging to replace due to scarcity. Restorers now use different types of stone, resulting in a gradual shift to a darker hue.
  • Honest Vandals: In 2010, graffiti artists defaced the statue, leading to their self-surrender to authorities. The then-mayor deemed it a "crime against the nation".
  • Catholic Church's Contribution: The Catholic Church in Brazil donated the funds that entirely financed the construction of Christ the Redeemer

Remember, this colossal statue not only stands as a symbol of faith but also offers breathtaking views of Rio de Janeiro and Guanabara Bay!
