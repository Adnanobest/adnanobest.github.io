Petra
Introduction Key Facts Iconic Features Modern Exploration Interesting Facts

Introduction

  • Petra is an archaeological wonder nestled in the southern part of Jordan.
  • It was the center of an Arab kingdom during Hellenistic and Roman times.
  • The city is famous for its stunning rock-cut architecture and historical significance.

Key Facts

  • Location: Southwest Jordan, in the Wadi Musa (Valley of Moses).
  • History: Built on a terrace, Petra was a thriving city for centuries.
  • Water Source: According to tradition, Moses struck a rock here, and water gushed forth.

Iconic Features

  • The Treasury (Al-Khazneh) : A magnificent rock-cut facade adorned with intricate carvings. Featured in movies like “Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade.” A symbol of Petra's grandeur.
  • The Monastery (Ad Deir): Another impressive rock-cut structure. Requires a challenging hike to reach. Offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Siq: A narrow, winding gorge leading to Petra. Flanked by towering cliffs. An awe-inspiring entrance to the city.

Modern Exploration

  • Petra attracts travelers from around the world.
  • UNESCO World Heritage-listed site.

Interesting Facts

  • Ageless Beauty: Petra is one of the oldest cities in the world, founded around 312 BC as the capital of the Nabatean Empire. With over 2300 years of history, it stands as a testament to human ingenuity and endurance.
  • UNESCO Heritage Site: The Petra Archaeological Park is one of Jordan's five UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Its intricate carvings and mysterious past continue to captivate travelers
  • Etymology: The name “Petra” itself means “rocks”. Appropriately, this city hewn from stone was named after the Greek word “Petros”, signifying rock.
  • Original Name: Among its Nabatean inhabitants, Petra was known as “Raqemo”, as suggested by inscriptions on its walls. This alternative name reflects its royal heritage.
  • Royal Resting Place: Petra's intricately sculpted tombs, including the iconic Urn Tomb, were the final resting places of Nabatean royalty. Carved between the 1st century BC and the 2nd century AD, these monuments stand as a testament to wealth and importance.
  • Lost City: Petra is often called “The Lost City”. Although its exact founding date remains unknown, it thrived from the 1st century BC until approximately 363 AD. An earthquake in the 4th century led to its abandonment for over five centuries.
  • Blushing Pink Hue: The mesmerizing pink color of Petra comes from the type of stone it was built into: limestone. No wonder it's also known as the “Red Rose City”

Petra's allure continues to inspire adventurers and history enthusiasts alike. Unleash your inner Indiana Jones and explore this ancient wonder!
